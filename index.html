Sharey
The aim of this project was to understand "What do people do with their excess food? and how can we help?"
Drawing from our research findings, we learned that individuals who regularly cook are dissatisfied with the amount of food waste they produce and would be more likely to donate excess food if a user-friendly platform to facilitate donations were available.
Project Overview
User problem -Based on our research, we discovered that individuals who cook on a regular basis are dissatisfied with the quantity of food waste they generate and would be more inclined to donate extra food if there was a user-friendly platform to make donations seamless and possible.
Solution - How might we develop a solution/system that allows frequent cooks to donate extra food, so that we can increase a higher percentage of food donation to food banks.

Research
For our user research we began by conducting an interview plan to gain a better understanding of our potential users. Some of the question were a bit challenging due to the nature of the topic on food crisis and during our research we also discovered that how sensitive the question was going to be, however we all remained professional throughout by first asking, our mentor to look over some of the question in our research plan. After an approval from our lecturer, we decided to continue with some of question and started conducting interviews.We also created a survey for family and friends to answer similar questions to the interviews, which was really helpful with our findings. We discovered that of the majority of participants would give more to those if there was an easier way to do so.
Majority of participants would give more to those in need, if they found an easier route of access.
Almost half had never donated to a food bank before. These insights helped us to identify a gap in the market.Interview TranscriptsHere are some of the insights we gained interviewing our peers, during this we also discovered pain points that people experience when they are thinking donating.
For our users, self satisfaction and a sense of responsibility were driving factors in helping those in need. They wanted to know how their help is impacting those who need it.Affinity DiagramFrom those findings, we created bullet points and synthesised an affinity diagram, which gave our user insights for our empathy map.

Ideation
Our approach was to all come up with number of generated ideas of what our user insight may sound and this was more close to our objective for this project.
Using the "I like, I wish, what if! tool as our starting point, we generated ideas from each and voted for the best ideas with the most stamps or agreeable. We then began the ideation process to come up with ideas for the our, once the user persona and problem statement was established for this project.
We created feature prioritisation matrix to help us synthesise our favourite ideas and based on this, we had picked the ideas from low complexity and high priority.
The value proposition was then introduced to gain trust and companionship by simply sharing food with others in need.
StoryboardThe storyboard was created based on Susan and her first time encountering the app Sharey after going through a stressful time with having excess food without knowing what to do with.
She asks her neighbour if she would like it. She’d already eaten. She then calls her sister to see if she wants it.
However, she comes across her best friend who recommends Sharey to her, saying that she has been using this app for a while now and that it is seamless to use.
Susan is upset that she is about to throw away half a lasagne that she spent most of the day preparing.
Susan goes home and downloads Sharey. She now has access to nearby food banks and closest drivers available to pick their food.
Susan clicks on the closest driver available for collection and choses the time for collection too as the app gives you options for more flexibility.
Once the driver has collected her lovely meal, Susan was ultimately satisfied with both her experience with the service and within herself by not throwing away meals.
Here, we have mapped out Susan’s experience, starting with her food excess,  all the way through to her positive experience with Sharey, and telling her friends about it. As you can can visually see her emotions throughout the journey, ending on a high.
The user flow was created using the scenario. The main focus from this group was to design a seamless user flow for the user from start to the end.
After the user visits the main screen they can get a better understanding of the application and navigate through to their messages and are able to share a meal. Also other features such as their ratings if interested.

The user is then met with a decision where they can decide to either drop of the food themselves at local food bank or choose to have someone collect it for them.

If the food needs to be collected then the users information would be collected, they would be instructed how to package the meal for the delivery - then it would be collected.

Prototyping
I carefully analysed the design system and all of its components through the process of an heuristic evaluation on four different websites. Important insights were obtained from this assessment regarding features that are required, such as noticeable Call-to-Action (CTA) buttons on the main page such as "Volunteer" and "Donate". Additionally, the evaluation included a breakdown of the benefits. disadvantages, and competitive advantages of each website. Interestingly, one common weakness found in the majority of websites was lacking of mobile application, which suggests an opportunity for industry distinction and improvement.

When creating our original paper wireframes we thought a points based system would be a great feature for the user, but insights from our user testing made it apparent that this would not align with our app’s concept and value proposition.
